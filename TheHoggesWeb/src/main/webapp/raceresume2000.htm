Rick Hogge

PERSONAL

 

I started road racing in 2000 but have had a passion for motorcycling for many years.  I rode dirt bikes for years but after my first track day while attending Keith Code’s class I was hooked on the adrenaline and speed.  Shortly after moving to Austin, Texas in 1999, I decided to get involved with road racing as a novice with the CMRA.  I met several experienced riders who helped me to exceed my first year expectations. 

CMRA Info

 

The Central Motorcycle Roadracing Association (CMRA) is the south central affiliate for the national PACE/CCS racing organization.  Currently, the CMRA racing season runs from February to November and holds events at four tracks in Texas and Oklahoma.  Past CMRA members include champions Colin Edwards, Kevin Schantz and Nicky Hayden.

bike

 

My bike is a 2000 Yamaha R6 with very limited modifications; these include a  Factory jet kit, M4 slip-on exhaust, Scotts steering damper and RaceTech fork springs.  The only mechanical problem I encountered this year was a broken shift linkage.  I have also had occasions to test the crash-worthiness of the R6 and I have been pleased with it durability.

goals

 

The CMRA expert classes will provide much more competition in the upcoming season.  I will learn from the faster riders and do whatever necessary to excel each weekend.  I would like to gain sponsorship and build relationships with companies in order to promote their products.  I hope such relationships will offset some of the expenses of motorcycle road racing and establish my corporate identity in the racing community.
